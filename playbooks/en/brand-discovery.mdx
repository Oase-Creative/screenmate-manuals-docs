## Overview

This playbook describes the complete internal process for executing a Brand Discovery journey. The goal is to gain insight into the target audience, brand identity, visual direction, and strategic positioning of the client.

## Step 1: Check the intake form

- Check if the client has completely filled out the form.
- Check if all required materials are present:
  - Existing branding (if available)
  - Target audience information
  - Competitors
  - Any wishes or moodboards

<Warning>
**Important:** Are data missing? Then first contact the client before proceeding.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Contact the client

- Always use your business ...@oasecreative.nl email address.
- Confirm the appointment (online or on location).
- Send a short agenda of the session.

## Step 3: Execute Discovery Session

- Go through the brand analysis:
  - Target audience
  - Problem & solution
  - Brand personality
  - Tone of voice
  - Competition
- Collect input for the visual direction:
  - Colors
  - Typography
  - Moodboards
  - Inspiration images

## Step 4: Develop Stylescape

- Open a new project in Photoshop or Figma.
- Consider:
  - 3000Ã—3000px artboards
  - Neatly organized layers
- Build 1 or 2 stylescapes based on the Discovery input.

## Step 5: Prepare Presentation

- Export stylescapes under 10MB per image.
- Add notes about:
  - Colors
  - Direction
  - Branding choices

## Step 6: Present to Client

- Discuss both directions.
- Note revisions or preferences.
- Update the team in Slack with the feedback.

## Step 7: Deliver Brand Discovery Document

The final document contains:
- Target audience description
- Brand personality
- Core values
- Positioning
- Stylescape(s)
- Recommendations for branding

## Best Practices

<Tip>
- Maintain a consistent structure in files.
- Communicate timely via Slack with the team.
- Process client wishes accurately (for example, color preferences).
</Tip>

## Workflow Summary

1. Check intake form  
2. Contact client  
3. Discovery session  
4. Develop stylescape  
5. Prepare presentation  
6. Present to client  
7. Deliver final document
